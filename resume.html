Tufts JumboCode

hello

JumboCode is a student-run group that develops software for non-profit organizations. I had volunteered at homeless shelters and food banks and had experienced some of the inconveniences of keeping track of supplies, so I was extremely happy to work for the Harvard Square Homeless Shelter team to make the process easier using technology. I focused on the admin website which allows admins to easily oversee supplies, volunteers, interactions with guests, and formal reports to the boss. I started with designing the sign in page using JSX, sign in users with Firebase. Then I designed a component that allows admin users to create admin users each with an email login and password. I changed to sign in rules so that only verified admin users can log in to the website and see supply data. My main challenge was to design the supplies page which shows 15 regular supplies along with supplies admins added for the day. The volunteers document each interaction with homeless guests on the mobile application and check the supplies they gave out to each guest. The website syncs with the app and displays the data for admins to make the end-of-day reports easier. This is the first time where my code is going to be used by real-life organizations. I learned to be extremely cautious about the functional correctness about my code and to be very deadline oriented. Our team communicated with the homeless shelter constantly to make sure they are aware of the design and delivered weekly presentations to the rest of JumboCode to update on our progress, which dramatically improved my communication skills.

  • Created new functionality to separate administration and volunteer's login
  • Designed and implemented supply page to allow volunteers select regular aid items along with adding in new items before volunteering begins
  • Presented to team updates on the project weekly

China Telecom Americas

I worked as a business operations intern during my freshman year summer at China Telecom Americas, a small company in Virginia focusing on cell phone plans for the Chinese community here in the US. This was not a very technical role, but it offered me a lot of operations, management, and leadership experience. I worked as a member of the technical customer support team in the beginning to learn about the company's daily business operations and to familiarize myself with a variety of products CTA offered. I realized there were some inefficiencies in training new customer support team members and communications between Chinese and American customer support teams so some tickets bounce between them without getting resolved. I started to write a curriculum to clarify the responsibilities of each team and the standard, improved procedure for handling different common issues. This curriculum became one of the essential guides for employees in the newly launched Canadian branch and aided the company to clear out customer support requests every day during the time of my internship. I also took on a side project of building a simple demonstration of using USPS API to detect incorrect addresses because I had noticed a lot of tickets reflect our customers filling out shipping addresses incorrectly because of the difference in address formats between the US and China and because I wanted to gain exposure in web programming. After I proposed the idea to my manager and showed her the demo, she forwarded the idea and the feature was eventually built by the engineering team in China.

  • Upgraded shipping system with USPS API
  • Lead the customer support team to best performance in company history through designing new curriculum
  • Oversaw employee database, cut company expenses by 10k
